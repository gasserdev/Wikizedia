IP address

An IP address is a unique identifier assigned to every device connected to
the internet or a local network. It allows devices to communicate and transfer data efficiently.

Types of IP Addresses

  1. Public IP Address – Used to identify devices on the internet.

  2. Private IP Address – Assigned within a local network (e.g., home or office).

  3. Dynamic IP Address – Changes periodically and is assigned by an ISP.

LAN Network

A Local Area Network (LAN) is a network that connects devices within a limited geographical area, such as a home, office, or campus.
It allows devices to communicate and share resources like printers and files.

Properties of LAN

  1. High Speed – LANs typically offer high data transfer rates.

  2. Limited Range – LANs cover a small geographical area.

  3. Low Cost – Setting up a LAN is generally cost-effective.

WAN Network

A Wide Area Network (WAN) is a network that covers a large geographical area, such as a city, country, or even the entire globe.
It connects multiple LANs and allows long-distance communication.

Properties of WAN

  1. Large Coverage – WANs can span vast distances.

  2. Lower Speed – WANs generally have lower data transfer rates compared to LANs.

  3. Higher Cost – Setting up a WAN can be expensive due to infrastructure requirements.
